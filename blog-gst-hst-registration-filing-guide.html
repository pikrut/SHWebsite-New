GST/HST Registration & Filing: When You Need It and How to Do It

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) and Harmonized Sales Tax (HST) are consumption taxes that apply to most goods and services sold in Canada. If you run a business, understanding when to register, how to collect, and how to file is critical for staying compliant with the CRA.

The Small Supplier Threshold: $30,000

You must register for GST/HST if your total taxable revenue exceeds $30,000 in a single calendar quarter or over four consecutive calendar quarters. Once you exceed it, you must register within 29 days.

Even below the threshold you may want to register voluntarily — registered businesses can claim Input Tax Credits (ITCs) to recover the GST/HST paid on business purchases.

Understanding HST Rates Across Canada

The rate depends on where the supply is made. Ontario charges 13% HST, the Atlantic provinces charge 15% HST, and Alberta, BC, Saskatchewan, Manitoba, and the territories charge 5% GST.

For businesses serving clients across provinces — like SH Elevate serving clients from Thunder Bay to Nova Scotia — understanding place-of-supply rules is essential.

Input Tax Credits (ITCs): Getting Your Money Back

ITCs let you recover the GST/HST you paid on purchases used in commercial activities. To claim them you need proper documentation: the supplier's name, GST/HST number, date, amount paid, and tax charged.

Common ITC-eligible expenses include office supplies, equipment, professional services, vehicle expenses (business portion), rent, and utilities.

Filing Frequencies: Annual, Quarterly, or Monthly

The CRA assigns filing frequencies based on annual revenue. Taxable supplies of $1.5 million or less can file annually; $1.5M–$6M file quarterly; over $6M file monthly. You can request more frequent filing to manage cash flow and claim ITCs sooner.
